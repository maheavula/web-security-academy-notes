WHAT THIS DOES
You upload your real meal photo / video frame, type in the numbers you looked
up, and the AI only does the stylish editing — it places a clean label on each
food item showing Protein, Fiber and Zinc, and adds a TOTAL badge. The AI does
NOT calculate anything; you give the numbers, so the values stay accurate.

WHICH TOOL TO USE (must be able to edit YOUR photo)
  • Google Gemini image edit ("Nano Banana")   — best
  • ChatGPT (upload the frame, ask it to edit)  — also great
  (A plain text-to-image tool like Midjourney can't annotate your real food.)

WHERE TO GET THE NUMBERS (free)
  • Cronometer  -> cronometer.com  (free, in browser; shows protein, fiber & zinc
    per item from the USDA database. For nuts/seeds pick the plain USDA entry.)
  • Quick single-item backup -> USDA FoodData Central -> fdc.nal.usda.gov
  Units: Protein & Fiber = grams (g).  Zinc = milligrams (mg).

THE PROMPT  (fill the [BRACKETS], then paste with your photo)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Using the meal photo I've uploaded, keep the photo exactly as it is — do not
change, move, recolour or regenerate the food, plate, lighting or background.
Only add a clean, stylish nutrient-label overlay on top of it for a vertical
9:16 Instagram fitness Reel. For each food item listed below, add a small modern
label connected to that item by a thin pointer line ending in a small dot, and
display EXACTLY these names and values without calculating or changing any
number — just show what I give you:

[ITEM 1 — e.g. 2 Boiled Eggs] — Protein [12g] · Fiber [0g] · Zinc [1.3mg]
[ITEM 2 — e.g. Almonds (15)] — Protein [6g] · Fiber [3g] · Zinc [0.9mg]
[ITEM 3 — e.g. Pumpkin Seeds (1 tbsp)] — Protein [3g] · Fiber [1g] · Zinc [2mg]
[ITEM 4 — e.g. Oats (40g)] — Protein [5g] · Fiber [4g] · Zinc [1.5mg]
[ITEM 5 — e.g. Apple] — Protein [0g] · Fiber [4g] · Zinc [0.1mg]

Put each label on a small rounded semi-transparent dark chip with the three
values in a neat single row (Protein, then Fiber, then Zinc), each value with a
tiny coloured dot before it (green for protein, amber for fiber, blue for zinc).
Use a bold, high-contrast sans-serif font (Poppins / Montserrat style) in white,
so it's readable on any background. Place every label in the empty space around
the food so it never covers the food itself, and never let two labels overlap.
In the top-right corner add one bold rounded "TOTAL" pill showing the summed
totals I provide: TOTAL — Protein [26g] · Fiber [12g] · Zinc [5.8mg]. Use clean
green-and-white accents for a premium nutrition-app look. Keep a vertical 9:16
layout with all text inside the central safe zone (leave clear space at the very
top and very bottom so Instagram's buttons don't cover it). Modern, minimal,
crisp and professional.
